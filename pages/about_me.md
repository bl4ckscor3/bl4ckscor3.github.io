## Eyewire/FlyWire
[Eyewire](https://eyewire.org/explore) and [FlyWire](https://flywire.ai/) are research projects from Princeton University with the goal of mapping neural structures within a mouse and fruit fly respectively. As these are helped by citizen scientists, the websites are gamified to a certain extent and as such have a scoring system and leaderboard. I've contributed to both projects through proofreading and completing data, as well as helping out within the community and programming 3rd party quality of life addons for the website.

Some projects include [improving the visibility on the website's leaderboard](https://forum.eyewire.org/t/colorful-leaderboard-a-script-to-color-player-names-in-the-leaderboard/1951), or [adding sorting functionality](https://forum.eyewire.org/t/mystic-sorting-a-script-that-adds-sorting-buttons-to-the-mystic-scouts-log/1981) to a part of the game's work log.\
Find the rest of the addons I made for this project [on my GitHub](https://github.com/bl4ckscor3?tab=repositories&q=eyewire&type=&language=&sort=).

[![Nature Cover](/assets/img/nature-cover.jpg){: .resize}](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07558-y)\
The research based on FlyWire has since been [published in the nature magazine.](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07558-y)
